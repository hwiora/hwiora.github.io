About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute of Neuroinformatics, ETH Zurich, working at the intersection of audio machine learning and computational neuroscience. My research develops scalable models and data workflows for real-world audio and biological signals, spanning representation learning, foundation model adaptation, large-scale audio data curation, and generative modeling. I combine quantitative audio analysis with computational modeling to study complex vocal signals and developmental change.

Published Applied Sciences, 2026

Large-Scale Audio Representation Workflow and Human-in-the-Loop Clustering

Developed a semi-automated workflow using Whisper encoder embeddings, clustering, and interactive expert review to organize real-world vocalization data. On a benchmark of 1,454 zebra finch vocalizations, the workflow achieved ARI = 0.73 and NMI = 0.81, while reducing annotation time by approximately 50%.

Applied the workflow to approximately 80,000 hours of recordings for downstream doctoral research.

Published ICASSP, 2024

WhisperSeg: Whisper-Based Segmentation for Human and Animal Vocal Activity

Adapted a pretrained Whisper Transformer for long-context, token-based voice activity detection, generating onset, offset, and vocalization-type tokens from spectrograms across human and animal recordings.

Achieved segment-wise F1 = 0.9966 on adult zebra finch recordings and outperformed frame-wise baselines across evaluated species, including noisy marmoset and human recordings.

Co-author; contributed to dataset construction and baseline evaluation, and subsequently applied segmentation outputs in large-scale analysis workflows.

Counterfactual Audio Generation and Trajectory Variance

Designed and implemented an age-conditioned counterfactual model using VAE latent representations and mini-batch optimal-transport matching, with single-forward-pass latent displacement prediction.

Evaluated on approximately 680K vocalizations across three zebra finches; the model outperformed Gaussian OT, per-age k-NN, and per-age OT baselines, with Cohen's d = 0.29–0.57 and AUC = 0.58–0.67 after controlling for duration.

Conference Abstract COSYNE, 2024

Computational Modeling of Vocal Template Formation

Investigated how juvenile zebra finches form composite song templates from sequential tutor exposure. Developed a Bayesian causal-inference model fitted to behavioral data from 19 juvenile birds and 26 syllables.
