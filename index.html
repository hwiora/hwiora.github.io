About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute of Neuroinformatics, ETH Zurich, working at the intersection of audio machine learning and computational neuroscience. My research focuses on large-scale real-world vocalization data, learned audio representations, data curation and annotation workflows, and generative modeling of developmental change. I develop computational methods for analyzing biological acoustic signals, including human-in-the-loop clustering workflows and counterfactual modeling over latent audio representations. I am also interested in adjacent applied research problems where robust modeling of real-world acoustic or biological signals is central.

Published Applied Sciences, 2026

Scalable Semi-Automated Clustering for Vocalization Data

Developed a workflow combining Whisper-derived embeddings, clustering, and expert proofreading for vocalization analysis. On a benchmark of 1,454 zebra finch vocalizations, the semi-automated workflow achieved ARI = 0.73 and NMI = 0.81, while reducing annotation time by approximately 50% relative to fully manual annotation.

The workflow is also being applied within my doctoral research to approximately 80,000 hours of recordings.

Trajectory Variance: An Unsupervised Measure of Developmental Vocal Plasticity

Developed an age-conditioned model over VAE latent representations that predicts counterfactual developmental shifts using mini-batch optimal-transport matching. The method measures predicted vocal change across developmental ages without requiring vocalization-type labels.

Evaluated on three zebra finches with 183K–274K vocalizations per bird, trajectory variance separated song syllables from calls after controlling for duration (Cohen's d = 0.29–0.57; AUC = 0.58–0.67).
