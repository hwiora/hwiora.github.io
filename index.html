About

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute of Neuroinformatics, ETH Zurich, developing machine learning methods for large-scale acoustic data and vocal behavior. My research spans representation learning, foundation model adaptation, and generative modeling, with work on Whisper-based segmentation, human-in-the-loop clustering, and counterfactual analysis of developmental vocal plasticity. I developed and evaluated an audio curation workflow that reduced annotation time by approximately 50% on a 1,454-vocalization benchmark and has been applied to approximately 80,000 hours of recordings. My work is grounded in computational neuroscience and aimed at robust, scalable modeling of real-world audio.

Trajectory Variance for Counterfactual Modeling of Vocal Development

“Trajectory Variance: An Unsupervised Measure of Developmental Vocal Plasticity.” Accepted to Interspeech 2026.

Designed and implemented an age-conditioned counterfactual model using VAE latent representations and mini-batch optimal-transport matching, with single-forward-pass latent displacement prediction.

Evaluated on approximately 680K vocalizations across three zebra finches; the model outperformed Gaussian OT, per-age k-NN, and per-age OT baselines, with Cohen's d = 0.29–0.57 and AUC = 0.58–0.67 after controlling for duration.

Manuscript Code

Large-Scale Audio Representation Workflow and Human-in-the-Loop Clustering

“Benchmarking Automated and Semi-Automated Vocal Clustering Methods.” Applied Sciences, 2026.

Developed a semi-automated workflow using Whisper encoder embeddings, clustering, and interactive expert review to organize real-world vocalization data. On a benchmark of 1,454 zebra finch vocalizations, the workflow achieved ARI = 0.73 and NMI = 0.81, while reducing annotation time by approximately 50%.

Applied the workflow to approximately 80,000 hours of recordings for downstream doctoral research.

Paper Code

WhisperSeg: Long-Context Voice Activity Detection for Human and Animal Recordings

“Positive Transfer of the Whisper Speech Transformer to Human and Animal Voice Activity Detection.” ICASSP, 2024.

WhisperSeg adapts a pretrained Whisper Transformer for long-context, token-based voice activity detection, generating onset, offset, and vocalization-type tokens directly from audio spectrograms across human and animal recordings.

Achieved segment-wise F1 = 0.9966 on adult zebra finch recordings and outperformed frame-wise baselines in segment-wise evaluation across all tested datasets, including noisy marmoset and human recordings.

Co-author; developed dataset-processing and baseline-evaluation components for the study, then applied segmentation outputs in subsequent large-scale analysis workflows.

Paper

Computational Modeling of Vocal Template Formation

“Serial Tutoring Reveals a Composite Song Template.” COSYNE, 2024.

Investigated how juvenile zebra finches form composite song templates from sequential tutor exposure. Developed a Bayesian causal-inference model fitted to behavioral data from 19 juvenile birds and 26 syllables.
